Bio

I am Rodrigo Loza, a Biomedical engineer with great interest in the application of representation learning in the medical field.

On the professional side, I am a co-founder of Sinapsis, a Bolivian-startup that uses Deep Learning models to automate microscopy diagnostics. My role in the company is to design, build and code the whole information system. I developed the Android applications, the frontend, the backend, the embedded software and, of course, the Deep Learning models.

Previously, I was an undergraduate research assistant at the Computer Engineering Laboratory - UCB working on Deep Learning in Computer Vision and embedded hardware (FPGAs). During undergraduate studies I managed to obtain a research scholarship and succeed in multiple local and oversea hackathons from important organizations/companies like UCB La Paz - Bolivia, The american embassy, Mojix and The Hackaday prize. As I briefly mentioned before, in 2016 I managed to succed in 3/5 stages of The Hackaday Prize 2016 and obtained USD $5000 to fund my startup. Later that year, I searched for a bigger seed investment and managed to work alongside Franz Freudenthal in the early stage of the startup.

On a side for fun I teach, mentor, work on pet projects and read about startups. I love to hang out with people interested in AI and I am always open to Zoom, email or just text with people that would like to discuss my work or AI in general.
